Techability Coding School

Techability Inc.

Unity Curriculum Developer

Techability Inc. is an entirely online school for K-12 students to learn programming. Curriculum at Techability is divided at the 4th grade level, where students below this grade level are taught in MIT's Scratch platform, while the remaining students are taught in Unity. Each curriculum unit spans one month, in which students spend their time in class learning how to use their respective tools and how to apply programming concepts to make games. Students are also asked to follow along with a new tutorial series, posted each month, that walks the student through creation of a complete game project.

Phase Shift

2020-2021

Full Mechanical Prototype

This VR project was built to test the limitations of motion in VR. This project centered around the concepts of player motion that feel the most fun to play with in flat-screen, first-person games--grappling hooks and bunnyhopping. It features a fully fleshed out system for climbing, swinging, jumping, and combat. It was iterated on several times, many of which featured a combination of hand-held daggers, which could be thrown and teleported to while in motion. Ultimately, the final concept revolved around empty hands, which would enable the user to interact more easily with the objects in their environment.

First Person Football League (FPFL)

2020 - 2021

Proof of Concept

First Person Football League was a proof of concept game, used to explore the idea of an arcade-style American Football game, played entirely from the first-person perspective. This game featured AI receivers, whose routes were chosen by the user during the pre-play phase, and would be run using simple pathfinding. Players could throw the football at varying angles with varied power, by holding the left-mouse button for a range of time. This game featured a basic multiplayer implementation, allowing users to directly connect to one another to play head-to-head.

Involvement Timeline:

June 2020 - January 2021

Job description:

This project was largely inspired by the Counter-Strike 1.6 gameplay mod, known as American Football Mod. It borrows the throwing mechanic and movement types seen present in this mod. All systems present here were built by me, and multiplayer systems were tested by myself and a close friend. Gameplay was fun, however due to art limitations and a lack of time, this project did not make it far past the prototyping phase. I have revisited this concept a number of times, and still believe this concept of first-person football could be quite popular!

FPS Platformer

2023

Mechanical Prototype

The FPS Platformer is a concept born from the Half-Life speedrun community, where Bunnyhopping is the through-line for the entire speedrun. Bunnyhopping in this project is directly derived from the movement systems in Half-Life 1, where air-strafing allows for player acceleration, and only frames spent on the ground will slow the player down. This game is built on top of the Unity FPS sample project, and centered around maps featuring small platforms, which the player must bunnyhop between to maintain their momentum and clear gaps. A finished version of this prototype would feature common 3D platformer tropes, including hidden gems, platforming based enemies, and speed-trials. It also centered around weapons which could assist the player both in combat and navigating tight platforming sections.

Wintermaul

2023-2024

Proof of Concept

Wintermaul is a modern adaptation of the classic Warcraft 3 custom-game by the same name. It is a co-op tower defense game, in which the player builds towers directly in the path of the creep hordes, forcing these creeps to navigate the towers, meaning the structure by which towers are placed directly contributes to the success of your build. This game currently features a grid based consturction system, dynamic AI navigation, and a dynamic tower inventory. It will eventually feature rogue-lite elements and complete multiplayer co-op!

Involvement Timeline:

November 2023 - Ongoing

Job description:

Wintermaul was a favorite custom game of mine during my time playing Warcraft 3, and I was quite dissapointed to find that no-one had revisited the concept in the modern games era. I decided to jump at the oppurtinity, with hopes to release the game, in full, at some point in the near future. This game is still very early in development, so a lot of major features are still missing, but my own love of the concept joined with my close friends love of Tower Defense games, has kept my motivation alive to continue pursuing this project. I hope for it to become my first full-release!
